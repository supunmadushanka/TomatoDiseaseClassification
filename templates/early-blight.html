Tomato Early Blight

Introduction

Tomato early blight is a fungal disease caused by the fungus Alternaria solani. It is a common disease that affects tomato plants in many parts of the world, particularly in warm and humid environments. The disease primarily affects the leaves of the tomato plant, causing brown lesions that can lead to defoliation and reduced yield. Control measures for tomato early blight include the use of resistant tomato varieties, fungicides, and cultural practices such as crop rotation and good sanitation.


Identification
  • Symptoms of tomato early blight include brown lesions on the leaves, which may have a target-like appearance with concentric rings of different colors
  • Lesions may also appear on the stem and fruit of the tomato plant
  • Infected leaves may become yellow and eventually fall off, leading to defoliation
  • Under humid conditions, a fuzzy growth of the fungus may be visible on the lesions

Managing and Treatment
  • Use resistant tomato varieties that have been bred to resist early blight
  • Use fungicides to control early blight; follow label instructions and use a fungicide rotation to prevent the development of resistance
  • Cultural practices such as crop rotation and good sanitation can help to reduce the incidence and severity of early blight
  • Remove and destroy infected plant material to prevent the spread of the disease
  • Practice good sanitation by removing weeds and debris that may harbor the fungus
  • Maintain proper spacing between plants to promote good air circulation and reduce humidity
  • Avoid overhead irrigation to prevent water from sitting on the leaves, which can promote fungal growth
